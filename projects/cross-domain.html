Cross-Domain

Ongoing  ·  Solo  ·  Knowledge Formalisation  ·  Automated Discovery

Inspired by Don Swanson and literature-based discovery, I am researching the classification of knowledge and how unrelated research questions might be connected to answers that already exist somewhere else in the literature. Swanson has already showed in the 1980s that published papers in completely separate fields, never citing each other, could be chained together to produce real, testable hypotheses.

Another part of what drew me in was watching GPT resolve supposedly-open Erdős problems, not by proving anything new, but by surfacing solutions already published in the literature that nobody had connected to the question.

The project asks whether knowledge can be classified systematically, and whether those classifications can reveal connections we would otherwise miss. Ongoing; architectural details are private.

Example connection

The hypothesis: the tardigrade Dsup protein physically shields DNA from hydroxyl-radical strand breaks. Radiotherapy kills cells through those same breaks. So transient Dsup expression in healthy tissue could raise the radiation dose a tumour can be given without protecting the cancer itself.

Both fields are real and active, yet no published paper connects the two. Illustrative; one example of the kind of link the project looks for.
