Cross-Domain

Ongoing  ·  Solo  ·  Knowledge Formalisation  ·  Automated Discovery

The core idea: scientific fields are silos. A result in condensed matter physics that resolves an open problem in oncology exists in the literature right now. Nobody has read both papers. Cross-Domain is a pipeline to find those connections automatically — by formalising the language of scientific publications into a unified representational structure and reasoning across fields.

The inspiration is Don Swanson, who showed in the 1980s that published papers in completely separate fields — never citing each other — could be chained by logical implication to produce real, testable hypotheses. He did it by hand, two hops at a time. The goal here is to do it at scale, across the full published record.

Ongoing. Architectural details are not public.
