Cross-Domain

Ongoing  ·  Solo  ·  Knowledge Formalisation  ·  Automated Discovery

Inspired by Don Swanson and literature-based discovery, I am researching the classification of knowledge: how unrelated research questions might be connected to answers that already exist somewhere else in the literature. Swanson showed in the 1980s that published papers in completely separate fields, never citing each other, could be chained together to produce real, testable hypotheses. He did it by hand, two hops at a time.

Part of what drew me in was watching GPT resolve supposedly-open Erdős problems, not by proving anything new, but by surfacing solutions already published in the literature that nobody had connected to the question. The answers were out there; the gap was nobody had read both halves.

The project asks whether knowledge can be classified systematically, and whether those classifications can reveal connections we would otherwise miss. Ongoing; architectural details are private.
