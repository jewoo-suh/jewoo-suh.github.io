Pokémon Ecosystem Simulator

Closed  ·  Solo  ·  Agent-Based Simulation  ·  Ecological Modelling

An agent-based ecological simulation where over 1,000 Pokémon species are placed into a world of biomes and left to interact with no external direction. The simulation is conceptually Conway's Game of Life multiplied by Lotka–Volterra dynamics: simple local rules produce complex global behaviour.

Design

Every species' ecological traits — metabolism rate, reproductive strategy, predation targets, habitat preferences, movement speed — are derived computationally from the official game data. The simulation does not use the game's battle mechanics; instead, it extracts the underlying biological logic implied by each species' stats and type interactions and translates it into ecological parameters. A fast Pokémon with high attack and a carnivore typing becomes a predator; a slow, high-defence species with a plant typing becomes prey.

The world is divided into biomes that each species can inhabit with varying degrees of suitability. Species placed outside their preferred biome suffer metabolic penalties; those in their native biome thrive. Food chains, territorial competition, and reproductive dynamics all follow from these parameters without any scripting of specific interactions.

What emerges

Given a random starting distribution, the simulation consistently produces population booms and crashes characteristic of predator-prey cycles, followed by long periods of relative equilibrium. Some species go extinct; dominant predators occasionally collapse their own food supply and crash shortly after. Invasive species introduced into a stable ecosystem can trigger cascading extinctions.

None of this is programmed explicitly. It is an emergent consequence of the interaction between species-level parameters and the shared environment.
