Pokémon Ecosystem Simulator

Closed  ·  Solo  ·  Agent-Based Simulation  ·  Ecological Modelling

1,025 Pokémon species, a world of biomes, and traits derived from actual game data — then left to run. Metabolism, reproduction rate, predation targets, habitat preference: all computed from the game’s stats and type system rather than made up. A fast Pokémon with high Attack and a carnivore typing becomes a predator. A slow, high-Defence grass type becomes prey.

No interactions are scripted. Food chains, population booms, territorial collapses, and long-run equilibria emerge from the numbers alone. The interesting part: dominant predators occasionally grow their population so large they consume their own food supply and crash shortly after. Invasive species introduced into a stable ecosystem trigger cascading extinctions. None of this is programmed — it follows from the parameter interactions.
