Language Evolution Simulator

Ongoing  ·  Solo  ·  Genetic Algorithms  ·  Computational Linguistics

This is a project I have been building to see whether language can evolve on its own, with nothing designed in advance. Around a thousand agents start with no words, no grammar, and no sounds. The only thing they are rewarded for is communicating successfully, and over thousands of generations a real language grows out of nothing.

The language that emerges is genuinely structured. It has a sound system of 5 vowels and 12 consonants, a rigid word order that does grammatical work, an evidential system (words that mark whether the speaker witnessed something or only heard about it), cliticised tense, and a graded number system (singular, paucal, plural) that emerged by composition rather than by inventing new words. Given a transitive sentence, agents recover who did what to whom around 84% of the time. None of this was specified; all of it evolved.

Everything below is measured directly from the agents’ corpus, not hand-written. It is one representative run.
