Language Evolution Simulator

Ongoing  ·  Solo  ·  Genetic Algorithms  ·  Computational Linguistics

Agents start with no language. They produce arbitrary sounds and are selected entirely by how well those sounds let them communicate with other agents. No designed phonology, no target grammar, no reward signal telling the system what structure should look like. After enough generations, structure appears anyway.

Current results: a ~50-word lexicon with CVC phonology, fully compositional grammar, four emergent grammatical categories (number, degree, aspect, magnitude), evidentiality, and tense. 86% role-decoding accuracy across communicative scenarios. Compositionality wasn’t designed in; it emerged because it’s the memory-efficient solution when you need to communicate a large space of situations with a small signal inventory.

Ongoing. Current work is testing whether the grammatical categories generalise or fragment under a richer communicative environment, and characterising the transition from holistic to compositional signalling more precisely.
