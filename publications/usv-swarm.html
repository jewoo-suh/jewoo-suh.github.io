Overview

This paper describes the design and development of a command and control (C2) system for swarms of Unmanned Surface Vessels (USVs), verified using a digital twin framework. The work was conducted in collaboration with Palantir Technologies as part of LIG Defence & Aerospace's multi-domain unmanned system programme.

The digital twin approach allows the C2 architecture to be tested and validated in simulation before deployment on physical platforms. The paper details the system architecture, the digital twin verification methodology, and the results of simulation-based validation across a range of operational scenarios including swarm coordination, target tracking, and communication degradation.

Contribution

J. Suh contributed to the AI integration components of the C2 system, including the agent coordination logic and the integration of AI-driven decision support within the Palantir Foundry platform.
