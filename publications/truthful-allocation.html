Truthful Task and Resource Allocation in Leader–Follower Agent Organisations

J. Suh et al.
PRIMA 2026  ·  2026
In preparation

Overview

Multi-agent systems in which a leader agent allocates tasks and resources to follower agents face a fundamental incentive problem: followers may misreport their capabilities or preferences to obtain more favourable allocations. This paper studies the mechanism design problem of constructing allocation rules that make truthful reporting a dominant strategy for follower agents.

The leader–follower structure is common in AI agent frameworks, autonomous systems, and organisational AI: a coordinating agent distributes subtasks across a pool of specialised agents whose true capabilities are private information. The paper characterises the class of truthful mechanisms for this setting, proves impossibility results for certain desiderata combinations, and proposes mechanisms that achieve truthfulness alongside efficiency and fairness properties where possible.

Context

The work draws on algorithmic mechanism design and multi-agent systems theory, with motivating applications in AI agent orchestration frameworks and defence command-and-control systems where misreporting by sub-agents carries significant operational risk.
