Iterated Self-Evaluation Impossibility for Large Language Models

J. Suh et al.
IEEE Access  ·  2026
In preparation

Overview

Self-evaluation — having a model assess the quality of its own outputs — has become a widespread technique in LLM alignment and RLHF pipelines. This paper investigates the theoretical limits of iterated self-evaluation: what happens when a model evaluates its own evaluations, repeatedly, without external grounding?

The paper establishes formal impossibility results for a class of iterated self-evaluation procedures. Under mild assumptions about the model's evaluation function, the sequence of self-assessments either converges to a fixed point that is independent of the original output quality, or diverges. In neither case does iterated self-evaluation provide reliable information about ground truth.

Significance

The results have direct implications for any alignment or evaluation pipeline that relies on a model judging itself without external reference. Constitutional AI variants, self-refinement loops, and model-graded evaluation frameworks are all subject to the impossibility conditions derived here. The paper characterises which evaluation architectures escape the impossibility and which do not.
