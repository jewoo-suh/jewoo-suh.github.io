Iterated Self-Evaluation Impossibility for Large Language Models

J. Suh et al.
IEEE Access  ·  In preparation, 2026

Self-evaluation — having a model judge its own outputs — is widely used in alignment pipelines. This paper establishes formal impossibility results for iterated self-evaluation: under mild assumptions, repeated self-assessment either converges to a fixed point independent of actual output quality, or diverges. Either way it provides no reliable signal. The paper characterises which evaluation architectures escape the impossibility and which do not.
