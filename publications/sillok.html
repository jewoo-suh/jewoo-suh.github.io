.sillok: A Korean-Specific Lossless Text Encoding Format for Linguistically-Informed Compression and Long-Term Archival

J. Suh
Preprint, 2026

Unicode encodes each Hangul syllable block as a single code point, discarding its internal structure: every block is composed of an initial consonant, a vowel, and an optional final consonant. .sillok represents text at the jamo (phoneme) level instead, applying compression priors derived from Korean phonotactics and morphology. Not all jamo combinations are legal; the distribution of legal ones is highly skewed. A linguistically-informed model over jamo sequences compresses significantly better than a general-purpose byte-level model, while remaining fully lossless.

Named after the Joseon Wangjo Sillok, a centuries-long Korean archival project that prioritised fidelity above all else. The format shares the same design priorities: reproducibility, longevity, and self-describing decodability.
