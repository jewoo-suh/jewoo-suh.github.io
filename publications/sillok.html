.sillok: A Korean-Specific Lossless Text Encoding Format for Linguistically-Informed Compression and Long-Term Archival

J. Suh
Preprint, 2026

In this paper, we present .sillok: a lossless encoding for Korean text, built for long-term archival. The encoding mechanism for .sillok first breaks each Hangul syllable into its onset, vowel, and coda and then codes each position with a fixed, published Huffman table, storing a syllable in 9.46 bits on average against UTF-8’s 24. Numbers, embedded non-Hangul text, spacing, and document structure each get their own lossless representation via an ASCII escape procedure.

The motivation behind this paper was creating a durable yet storage-efficient archive for Korean news. It takes its name from the Joseon Wangjo Sillok (the daily royal annals of the Joseon Dynasty of Korea) and aims for the same property of outliving its own tools. Thus, a .sillok bitstream is meant to be recoverable from the bits alone, through knowledge of Korean phonology and frequency analysis, with no software decoder required.

On a custom-compiled Korean news corpus, it reduces records by 56.4% versus UTF-8, matching Brotli and beating gzip, zstd, and lzma, while being the only one of them reconstructible from the bitstream alone.
