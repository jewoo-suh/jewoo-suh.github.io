.sillok: A Korean-Specific Lossless Text Encoding Format for Linguistically-Informed Compression and Long-Term Archival

J. Suh
Preprint, 2026

.sillok is a lossless encoding for Korean text, built for long-term archival. It breaks each Hangul syllable into its onset, vowel, and coda and codes each position with a fixed, published Huffman table, storing a syllable in 9.46 bits on average against UTF-8’s 24. Numbers, embedded non-Hangul text, spacing, and document structure each get their own lossless representation.

What makes it unusual is the design goal: a .sillok bitstream is meant to be recoverable from the bits alone, through knowledge of Korean phonology and frequency analysis, with no software decoder required. It takes its name from the Joseon Wangjo Sillok (the daily royal annals that remain readable centuries later) and aims for the same property of outliving its own tools.

On held-out Korean news, it reduces records by 56.4% versus UTF-8, matching Brotli and beating gzip, zstd, and lzma, while being the only one of them reconstructible from the bitstream alone. Reference implementations in Python and C produce byte-identical output.
