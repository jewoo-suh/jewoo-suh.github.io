Designing the VG8: An FPGA-Based 8-bit Game Console for Comparative Analysis of 1980s Personal Computers and Game Consoles

J. Suh
MMathCompSci Thesis, University of Oxford, 2025

The VG8 is a custom 8-bit game console implemented in Verilog on an FPGA, designed to recreate the core hardware of 1980s home computers and game consoles — NES, Commodore 64, ZX Spectrum, Apple II — at the register-transfer level. Not emulation: the hardware components (CPU, PPU, sound, memory bus) are synthesised and deployed directly on the FPGA, giving cycle-accurate reproduction of original behaviour.

The thesis uses this as an empirical platform to compare the design decisions that separated the major 8-bit platforms. The aesthetic and technical characteristics that retrospectively define each system — the NES’s tile sprites, the C64’s SID sound, the Spectrum’s colour clash — were almost entirely consequences of specific hardware economies made at design time, not choices about what the machine should look or sound like.
