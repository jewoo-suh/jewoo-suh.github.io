About

I am an Oxford-trained mathematician and computer scientist, currently working as a Research Engineer at LIG Defence & Aerospace in Seoul, where I develop AI systems for autonomous unmanned platforms in collaboration with Palantir Technologies.

My research centres on the formal representation of knowledge: how to express what is known across different domains in a language precise enough to reason over, and to reveal connections that informal methods miss. The longer-run question I keep returning to is whether scientific discovery can be made computable — not retrieval, but inference across the published record as a whole.

On the side I build simulations: agent populations that evolve language from scratch, ecosystems where food chains and extinctions emerge from game data, statistical reconstructions of historical mysteries.

Language Evolution Simulator

Agents evolve communication from scratch with no designed phonology or grammar. Currently yields a fully compositional language: ~50-word lexicon, four emergent grammatical categories, evidentiality, tense, 86% role-decoding accuracy.

ongoing Genetic Algorithms

Voynich Decode Project

Reproduced all measured structural properties of the Voynich Manuscript via a ~200-unit combinatorial generative procedure requiring no semantic content, statistically falsifying the cipher hypothesis.
