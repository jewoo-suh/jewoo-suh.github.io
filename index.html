About

I am an Oxford-trained mathematician and computer scientist, currently working as a Research Engineer at LIG Defence & Aerospace in Seoul, where I develop AI systems for autonomous unmanned platforms in collaboration with Palantir Technologies.

My research centres on the formal representation of knowledge: how to express what is known across different domains in a language precise enough to reason over, and to reveal connections that informal methods miss. The longer-run question I keep returning to is whether scientific discovery can be made computable — not retrieval, but inference across the published record as a whole.

On the side I build simulations: agent populations that evolve language from scratch, ecosystems where food chains and extinctions emerge from game data, statistical reconstructions of historical mysteries.

Language Evolution Simulator

ongoing

Agents evolve communication from scratch with no designed phonology or grammar. Currently yields a fully compositional language: ~50-word lexicon with CVC phonology, four emergent grammatical categories, evidentiality, tense, and 86% role-decoding accuracy across communicative scenarios.

Genetic Algorithms  ·  Computational Linguistics  ·  Solo

Voynich Decode Project

closed

Reproduced all measured structural properties of the Voynich Manuscript via a ~200-unit combinatorial generative procedure requiring no semantic content. Provides statistical evidence that the manuscript is a content-free artefact, falsifying the cipher hypothesis.

Statistical Analysis  ·  Computational Linguistics  ·  Solo

Pokémon Ecosystem Simulator

closed

1,000+ Pokémon species placed in a world of biomes with ecologically-derived traits computed from real game data. Predator–prey cycles, population crashes, and long-run equilibria emerge without any scripting of specific interactions. Conway’s Game of Life × Lotka–Volterra dynamics.
