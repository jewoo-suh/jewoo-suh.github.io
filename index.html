About

Projects

Cross-Domain

ongoing

A pipeline for formalising the language of scientific publications into a unified representational structure, then connecting seemingly unrelated papers across fields to surface hidden answers to hard problems. Inspired by Swanson-style literature-based discovery, it builds a cross-field knowledge graph spanning medicine, physics, and engineering.

Knowledge Formalisation  ·  Automated Discovery  ·  Solo

Language Evolution Simulator

ongoing

A genetic-algorithm simulation of language emergence from scratch: agents produce sounds and evolve communication with no prior structure. Currently achieved a fully-emergent artificial language with deterministic compositional grammar, 86% role-decoding accuracy, a ~50-word lexicon with CVC phonology, and four emergent grammatical categories (number, degree, aspect, magnitude) with evidentiality and tense.

Genetic Algorithms  ·  Computational Linguistics  ·  Solo

Voynich Decode Project

closed

Statistical modelling of the Voynich Manuscript, a 15th-century illustrated codex written in an unknown script. Reproduced the manuscript's structural properties via a ~200-unit combinatorial generative procedure, providing statistical evidence that it is a content-free artefact and falsifying the cipher hypothesis.

Statistical Analysis  ·  Computational Linguistics  ·  Solo

Pokémon Ecosystem Simulator

closed

An agent-based ecological simulation where 1,000+ Pokémon species are placed into a world of biomes and left to interact. Ecologically-derived traits (metabolism, reproduction, predation) computed from real game data; food chains, population booms, crashes, and equilibria emerge organically. Conceptually: Conway's Game of Life × Lotka–Volterra dynamics.
