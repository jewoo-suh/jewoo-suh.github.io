About

I graduated from the University of Oxford, Christ Church College in 2025 with a Master of Mathematics and Computer Science. I am currently a Research Engineer at LIG Defence & Aerospace, serving as part of my mandatory military service for the Korean government until July 2028. At work, I (1) am currently developing AI solutions for unmanned military systems in collaboration with Palantir Technologies, and (2) maintain and refine our internal framework, which provides core functionality across our company’s weapons systems.

My research interests live on the border between mathematics and computer science. I opt for a mathematical approach to CS problems, looking for mathematically ‘elegant’ representations of CS. Currently my interests split across two fields: knowledge representation (using maths and computation to find connections between separate topics) and genetic algorithms (using simple mathematical rules to grow elegant models and solutions).

Outside of work and academics, I enjoy travelling and eating, with my most recent trips being in Marrakech, Morocco and Oita/Beppu, Japan. I also spend a lot of free time building projects with Claude Code, and am currently working on creating an artificial language evolved through genetic algorithms and simulation (check below).

    J. Suh  ·  Preprint, 2026  ·  PDF
    A lossless Korean text format for long-term archival: 56.4% smaller than UTF-8, competitive with Brotli, and uniquely recoverable from the bitstream alone.
  5. 05
    Designing the VG8: An FPGA-Based 8-bit Game Console for Comparative Analysis of 1980s Personal Computers and Game Consoles
    J. Suh  ·  MMathCompSci Thesis, University of Oxford, 2025
    A simplified 8-bit game console built from scratch in Verilog on an FPGA, used as a platform to compare the hardware trade-offs of 1980s consoles and personal computers.

Language Evolution Simulator

ongoing

A simulation in which agents start with no language and evolve one through selection pressure alone. The current language consists of a 5-vowel, 12-consonant sound system, rigid word-order grammar, an evidential system, graded number, and ~84% who-did-what accuracy.

Genetic Algorithms  ·  Computational Linguistics  ·  Solo

Voynich Decode Project

suspended

Research into the undeciphered 15th-century Voynich Manuscript. Statistical fingerprinting that resists frequency-matching shows the text looks generated by a template, rather than a cipher for a natural language.

Statistical Analysis  ·  Computational Linguistics  ·  Solo
